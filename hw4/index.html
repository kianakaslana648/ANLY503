Questions:

(1) Can we find some difference of power consumption among different households as well as the time pattern?

(2) Can we create a plot in which the significant parts of power consumption for each households can be easily seen?

 

Designs:

(1) I choose to create an interactive panel of different power sources, whose components contain multiple lines for 3 households(If measured). The users can explore the consumption ways they are interested in by clicking on the drop-down button and choosing one. From each panel, they can also directly catch the difference of 3 households and make further inferences for the households from the time patterns.

(2) I choose to create an interactive panel which contain bar-plots of the power consumption percentages for 3 households. The users can directly choose one using the drop-down button and directly see the significant parts as well as the insignificant parts of power consumption for each household.

 

 

 

 

In the Fridge part of the figure, we can see that Household2 has the most power-consuming fridge while Household3 has the least power-consuming fridge. We can also see that in seasons during which the temperature is lower, the power consumption of fridges tends to be smaller.

We can also see that while almost for every day the fridges are used, appliances such as Coffee Machine and Kettle are not used everyday.

 

 

 

We can directly see that for Household1 the most significant part of power consumption is Freezer. For Household2 that’s Fridge. And for Household3 that’s Entertainment.
